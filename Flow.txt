THE FOLLOWING IS THE PROPER NAMING AND CATEGORIZATION OF THE ABOVE IDEA

2. Module 1: The Quest Board (Task Randomizer)
The goal is to eliminate "Decision Fatigue." Polymaths often spend more time choosing what to learn than actually learning.
A. Task Categorization

The Crown Jewel (Main Focus): A high-priority, long-term goal (Duration: 1 week to 1 month). This is a static mission that appears on the dashboard every day until completion.
Example: "Analog Electronics Masterclass" or "React.js Foundation."
The Side Hustles (Daily Randoms): A "pool" of smaller, recurring habits or interests.
Example: "15 mins Guitar," "3 Chess puzzles," "10 mins Spanish," "Quick Workout."

B. The Randomization Logic

The Daily Draw: Every morning, the app keeps the Crown Jewel active but "rolls the dice" to select 2 or 3 Side Hustles from the user's pool.
The "Reroll" Mechanic: If a user truly cannot perform a selected Side Hustle, they can "Reroll" once per day. However, doing so costs "Energy" (in-app currency), encouraging them to stick to the draw.

3. Module 2: The Hall of Kings (The Network)

A community for multipotentialites shouldn't just be a chat room; it should be a Resource Exchange.
A. The Town Hall Feed (Stat-Driven Discovery)
Global Learning Trends: A live feed showing what topics the community is currently "conquering." (e.g., "34% of Kings are currently building Programming Guilds").
Verified Resources: Instead of searching YouTube blindly, users can see the most "Log-linked" videos for a specific topic. If 50 people linked the same "Transistors 101" video in their progress logs, it rises to the top of the Town Hall.

B. Peer Review & Documentation

The Summary Exchange: When a King logs their daily progress, they write a brief "What I Learned" summary. These summaries can be made public in the Hall of Kings.
Knowledge Trading: Users can "follow" the learning journeys of others. If you are learning Guitar and someone else is 2 weeks ahead of you, you can look at their "Log History" to see which links and resources they used to get there.
